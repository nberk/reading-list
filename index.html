Reading List

Feeds I follow

Last updated March 1, 2026

Tech

  • One Useful Thing — A research-focused blog by Wharton professor Ethan Mollick exploring how AI is practically changing work and education.
  • Internal Tech Emails — A collection of internal documents and emails from major tech companies surfaced through legal discovery.
  • Simon Willison's Newsletter — A top-tier blog from an expert programmer, now mostly about engineering with AI.
  • Hardcore Software by Steven Sinofsky — A historical and strategic look at the PC industry and Microsoft's evolution by Steven Sinofsky, its former president of Windows.
  • Out of the Software Crisis (Newsletter) — Baldur Bjarnason's analysis of software development practices through the lens of systems thinking and engineering.
  • Tomasz Tunguz — Data-heavy analysis of venture capital, SaaS metrics, and technology market trends by Theory Ventures founder Tomasz Tunguz.
  • Intelligent Jello — Mike Gioia's commentary on generative AI and its impact on creative workflows for non-technical users.
  • Geoffrey Litt — Research and thoughts from Ink & Switch researcher Geoffrey Litt on malleable software and empowering users to program their own tools.
  • Last Week in AWS — Cloud economist Corey Quinn's weekly summary of news and updates from the Amazon Web Services ecosystem.
  • mandates — Ethan Ding's commentary on startup strategy and product-market fit from a founder's perspective.
  • Peter Steinberger — Notes from PSPDFKit founder Peter Steinberger on software engineering, indie development, and building products with AI.
  • karpathy — Andrej Karpathy's technical deep dives into neural networks and AI.
  • Martin Alderson — Technical benchmarks and experiments by Catch Metrics cofounder Martin Alderson on AI engineering and web frameworks.
  • Wonder Tools — Jeremy Caplan's brief reviews and recommendations of digital productivity and creativity tools.
  • Stratechery by Ben Thompson — Analysis of the business strategy and economic impact of major technology companies.
  • Platformer — Casey Newton's reporting on all things tech.
  • Essays - Benedict Evans — Long-form thoughts from independent tech analyst Benedict Evans on mobile, retail, and the long-term shifts in the tech industry.
  • TheNeuron — A daily summary of news and tools in the artificial intelligence industry by Pete Huang and Noah Edelman.
  • Ben's Bites — Ben Tossell's daily curation of new AI products, research papers, and industry news.
  • shruggingface.com — Jake Dahn's technical tutorials and thoughts on machine learning and AI development.
  • Rodney Brooks — Expert views from MIT professor emeritus and iRobot co-founder Rodney Brooks on the current state and future limitations of robotics and AI.
  • Interconnects AI — Nathan Lambert's technical explanations of how modern AI models are trained and evaluated.

Business

  • No Mercy / No Malice — Scott Galloway's weekly commentary on the digital economy and the winners and losers in modern capitalism.
  • Granted — Adam Grant's evidence-based insights on motivation, meaning, and psychology in the workplace.
  • Range Widely — David Epstein's exploration of how generalists and diverse skill sets lead to success in a specialized world.
  • Ben Casnocha — Thoughts on entrepreneurship, career management, and networking.
  • PAT KINSEL — Insights from Proof founder Pat Kinsel on venture capital and the process of building new companies.
  • A Smart Bear — Long-form essays by WP Engine founder Jason Cohen on the philosophy and tactics of building a profitable software business.
  • Sarah Tavel's Newsletter — Analysis of consumer behavior and marketplace dynamics from Benchmark partner Sarah Tavel.
  • Paul Graham: Essays — Influential essays from Y Combinator co-founder Paul Graham on startups, programming, and general intellectual curiosity.
  • David Senra — David Senra, host of the Founders podcast, distills lessons from the biographies of history's most successful entrepreneurs.
  • Michael Karnjanaprakorn's Newsletter — Thoughts from Skillshare co-founder Michael Karnjanaprakorn on the future of learning, work, and personal growth.
  • Clouded Judgement — Altimeter partner Jamin Ball's weekly analysis of public software company valuations and financial metrics.
  • Sajith Pai — Commentary from Blume Ventures partner Sajith Pai on the Indian startup ecosystem and venture capital trends.
  • Above the Crowd — Long-running blog by Benchmark partner Bill Gurley about the dynamics of the technology industry and financial markets.
  • The CQ — Analysis of consumer trends and the future of commerce from Forerunner Ventures.
  • Elad Blog — Tactical advice from entrepreneur and investor Elad Gil for scaling high-growth technology companies.
  • Harry Glaser — Personal reflections from Periscope Data co-founder Harry Glaser on leadership and the experience of being a tech CEO.
  • Khosla — Research and viewpoints on climate, health, and AI from Vinod Khosla's venture firm.
  • Ride It to the Sky — Thoughts from GroupMe and Fundera co-founder Jared Hecht on product development and the intersection of technology and culture.
  • What's Hot 🔥 in Enterprise IT/VC — Boldstart Ventures founder Ed Sim's summary of trends and funding activity in the enterprise software sector.
  • Novel Investor — CFP Jon Petersen's archive of investing wisdom, market history, and behavioral finance essays for long-term investors, running since 2010.
  • Collaborative Fund — Essays on business, culture, and long-term thinking from the Collaborative Fund team, featuring writing by Morgan Housel, author of The Psychology of Money.

Fun

  • Recomendo — A weekly newsletter from Kevin Kelly, Mark Frauenfelder, and Claudia Dawson with six brief recommendations for tools, books, and life hacks.
  • Forekast — Justin Kerby's calendar-based newsletter highlighting upcoming interesting events, holidays, and pop culture moments.
  • things magazine — A long-running blog focused on design, architecture, and visual culture.
  • Beautiful Public Data — Visual journalist Jon Keegan's blog exploring and visualizing interesting datasets found in the public domain.
  • OmarKnows — Recommendations for tools, books, and life hacks (from my former Microsoft colleague with great taste).
  • KK — The personal newsletter of Kevin Kelly, Wired co-founder and author, often focusing on his long-term projects and cool tools.
  • Nomadico — Tim Leffel's newsletter for frequent travelers and digital nomads, focusing on remote work and global living.
  • World of Reel — Jordan Ruimy's film criticism and festival coverage. Good round-up of entertainment industry happenings
  • Theme Park Insider — Robert Niles's news and vacation planning guide for theme and amusement parks worldwide, covering Disney, Universal, and beyond.

Food

  • Brand Eating — Daily fast food news, reviews, and deals — covering new menu items and limited-time offerings across major chains, written by one person since 2008.
  • The Impulsive Buy — Junk food and fast food reviews with a sense of humor, covering new and limited-edition snacks since 2003.
  • Food Business News — Trade publication covering food and beverage industry news, from product development and manufacturing to commodity markets.
  • Restaurant Dive — Industry Dive's in-depth journalism on the restaurant industry, covering consumer trends, operations, technology, and M&A.

Wellness

  • Oldster Magazine — Sari Botton's publication exploring the experience of getting older and the different stages of life.
  • Wait But Why — Tim Urban's deep-dive illustrated essays on science, philosophy, and human behavior.
  • A Working Library — Mandy Brown's blog about the practice of reading and the intersection of technology and literature.
  • The Technium — Kevin Kelly's blog exploring the global, integrated system of technology.
  • Age of Invention, by Anton Howes — Anton Howes's newsletter focused on the history of innovation and the Industrial Revolution.
  • Our World in Data — A data-driven publication founded by Max Roser, focusing on large-scale global problems like poverty, disease, and climate change.
  • Our World in Data - Data Insights — A data-driven publication from Max Roser's team, focusing on large-scale global problems like poverty, disease, and climate change.
  • The Library — A curated archive of channeled readings on consciousness, wellness, and spirituality from the collected works of Al Miner and Lama Sing.
  • Useful Fictions — Cate Hall's newsletter exploring how the stories and metaphors we use shape our understanding of the world.
  • Beautiful Voyager — Meredith Arthur's site dedicated to helping overthinkers, perfectionists, and people with anxiety.

Science

  • Experimental History — Adam Mastroianni's blog using psychological research and data to challenge common assumptions about human nature.

Government

  • Agglomerations — A newsletter from the Economic Innovation Group focusing on urban policy, housing, and how cities function.
  • The Argument — Jerusalem Demsas's newsletter focused on debating major political and social issues.
  • The Abundance and Growth Blog — Commentary from Matt Clancy and contributors at Coefficient Giving on policies and technologies that can drive economic growth and abundance.
  • Dylan Matthews — Vox senior correspondent Dylan Matthews writes on policy, poverty, and effective altruism.
  • Programmable Mutter — Johns Hopkins professor Henry Farrell's thoughts on political economy, technology, and governance.
  • Mike Murphy's Barking at the Moon Blabfest Substack — Political analysis and commentary from veteran GOP strategist Mike Murphy.
  • Eating Policy — Jennifer Pahlka's newsletter on state capacity, government effectiveness, and how to fix broken public institutions.

Link Aggregators

  • Weekly Diversions — Kevin Bratcher's weekly curation of interesting long-form articles from across the web.
  • Good Reading — Philip Ordway's curation of high-quality articles on business, finance, and life.
  • Marginal REVOLUTION — Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok's highly influential economics blog covering a wide range of academic and cultural topics.
  • Abnormal Returns — Daily curated links to the best investment and finance reading across the web.
  • The Big Picture — Barry Ritholtz's macro perspective on capital markets, economy, geopolitics, and behavioral finance. One of the most widely read financial blogs online.

Asia

  • Dan Wang — Annual essays and regular thoughts from Gavekal analyst Dan Wang on Chinese technology, manufacturing, and society.
  • The Scholar's Stage — Tanner Greer's essays on Chinese history, strategy, and international relations.
  • Zixuan Ma's Blog — Personal reflections and technical notes from a software engineer focused on Asian tech trends.
  • Concurrent — Afra Wang's newsletter on technology and geopolitics at the intersection of Silicon Valley and China.
  • Economy Class — A newsletter from Arjun Ramani, a journalist at The Economist, about economics, technology, and global trends.
  • The Tangled Woof — Andrew Batson, China research director at Gavekal Dragonomics, writes on Chinese economics, policy, and industrial development alongside interests in books, jazz, and history.

Law

  • cyber/data/privacy insights — Legal analysis from Cooley of emerging trends in cybersecurity, data protection, and privacy law.
  • Competitive Philosophy — Scott Stevenson's business strategy essays applying contrarian frameworks to product development, organizational design, and the impact of AI on the software industry.
  • The Redline by Version Story — Jordan Bryan's newsletter from Version Story on AI and version control tools reshaping the legal industry.
  • Privacy + Cyber + AI (Troutman Pepper Locke) — Legal analysis from Troutman Pepper Locke attorneys on privacy law, cybersecurity, and AI regulation.
  • FTC Press Releases — Official press releases from the Federal Trade Commission.
  • Jordan Furlong — Legal sector analyst on the structural shifts reshaping the legal profession, where it's headed, and what a better legal system could look like.
  • CodeX – Stanford Law School — Research and commentary from Stanford's CodeX Center for Legal Informatics on law, technology, and the future of legal practice.
  • 3 Geeks and a Law Blog — Long-running blog on legal technology, knowledge management, and law firm innovation, with an accompanying podcast, The Geek in Review.
  • Team Do Something — Sam Harden, attorney and developer, on law, AI, and building software that gets more justice to more people.
  • Legal Tech Trends — Peter Duffy's roundup of legaltech and AI market developments, informed by 10+ years of technology consulting with law firms and corporate legal teams.
  • The LegalTech Fund — Portfolio insights and industry updates from The LegalTech Fund, a venture firm backing early-stage legal technology startups.
  • Artificial Lawyer — Richard Tromans's news and analysis on legal AI and the transformation of the business of law.
  • LawSites — Robert Ambrogi's authoritative coverage of legal technology products and services, written by one of the longest-running legal tech journalists.
  • Off The Record — Alex Su, former lawyer turned tech exec, on legal operations, AI's impact on the industry, and the business of law.
  • Legal IT Insider — Caroline Hill's independent global coverage of legal technology.
